3-day itinerary Rio de Janeiro

Day 1: The Icons of Rio

Morning

  1. Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor)
  2. Take the train (Trem do Corcovado) from Cosme Velho station early in the morning to avoid crowds.
  3. Spend time enjoying the panoramic views and snapping photos.

Late Morning

  1. Santa Teresa Neighborhood
  2. Wander through the artsy streets, admire colonial mansions, and check out small galleries and cafes.
  3. Third step

Lunch

  1. Eat at Aprazível, a beautiful restaurant in Santa Teresa with amazing views of the city and Brazilian cuisine.

Afternoon

  1. Selarón Steps (Escadaria Selarón)
  2. Walk down to Lapa and visit the colorful tiled steps created by artist Jorge Selarón.

Evening

  1. Sugarloaf Mountain (Pão de Açúcar)
  2. Take the cable car up in time for sunset—one of the best views in Rio as the city lights up.

Dinner

  1. Have dinner in Urca at Bar Urca, a casual spot popular for sitting on the sea wall with drinks and seafood.

Day 2: Beaches & Nature

Morning

  1. Copacabana Beach
  2. Start your day with a walk along the iconic Copacabana beach promenade.
  3. Grab a breakfast at one of the beach kiosks.

Late Morning

  • Ipanema Beach & Arpoador Rock
  • Stroll to Ipanema and check out the vibrant beach culture.
  • Climb Arpoador Rock for views of the ocean and surfers.

Lunch

  • Try Zazá Bistrô Tropical in Ipanema for a relaxed meal with fresh, tropical flavors.

Afternoon

  • Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas
  • Rent a bike or pedal boat around the lagoon.
  • Stop at Parque Lage, a mansion turned art school with a lovely café and views of Corcovado.

Evening

  • Sunset at Ipanema
  • Find a spot near Posto 9 to watch the sun dip behind the mountains—a true Rio tradition.

Dinner

  • Head to Rua Dias Ferreira in Leblon for trendy restaurants. Try Jobi for casual Brazilian food or Oro (Michelin-starred) for a special night.

Day 3: Culture & Local Life

Morning

  1. Visit the Museum of Tomorrow (Museu do Amanhã)
  2. Explore this futuristic science museum in the revitalized Porto Maravilha area.
  3. Walk around the nearby Mauá Square and see the Metropolitan Cathedral.

Late Morning

  1. Visit the Rio Art Museum (Museu de Arte do Rio)
  2. Check out contemporary Brazilian art in this beautiful museum.
  3. Explore the surrounding area, including the Olympic Boulevard murals.

Lunch

  1. Eat at Confeitaria Colombo, a historic café in the city center known for its stunning architecture and pastries.

Afternoon

  1. Visit the Botanical Garden (Jardim Botânico)
  2. Stroll through the lush gardens, see the famous palm tree avenue, and visit the orchid house.
  3. Relax in the peaceful surroundings.

Evening

  1. Explore the nightlife in Lapa
  2. Head back to Lapa for dinner and to experience the vibrant nightlife.
  3. Check out the live music scene at one of the samba clubs or bars.

Dinner

  1. Try Rio Scenarium, a popular samba club with a great atmosphere and delicious Brazilian food.

"Rio de Janeiro is incredible, but like any big city, there are a few things to watch out for to stay safe and have a smooth trip." Here are 5 tips on what to avoid when visiting Rio:

1. Don’t Flash Valuables

  • Avoid walking around with expensive jewelry, flashy watches, or visibly holding your phone/camera.
  • Petty theft and pickpocketing can happen, especially on busy beaches or public transportation.
  • ✅ Tip: Keep valuables out of sight and use a crossbody bag or money belt.

2. Don’t Wander into Favelas Without a Guide

  • Some favelas are peaceful and even have tours, but others can be dangerous for outsiders due to crime or drug gang activity.
  • ✅ Tip: Only visit well-known favelas (like Vidigal or Rocinha) with a trusted local guide or tour company.

3. Don’t Go to the Beach at Night

  • Rio’s beaches are amazing during the day, but at night they can be risky with little security and poor lighting.
  • ✅ Tip: Enjoy the beach in daylight and stick to lively, well-lit areas after dark.

4. Don’t Rely Only on Cash

  • Carrying lots of cash is risky, but some places don’t accept foreign credit cards.
  • ✅ Tip: Bring a small amount of cash (for markets, taxis, etc.), but also have an international debit/credit card and use ATMs inside malls or banks.

5. Don’t Assume Everyone Speaks English

  • While staff in hotels or touristy areas often speak English, many locals don’t.
  • ✅ Tip: Learn a few basic Portuguese phrases like “obrigado” (thank you) and “por favor” (please) to make interactions smoother.
